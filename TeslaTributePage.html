NIKOLA TESLA

Serbian-American engineer and physicist Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) made dozens of breakthroughs in the production, transmission and application of electric power.
He invented the first alternating current (AC) motor and developed AC generation and transmission technology.
Nikola Tesla's work would go on to inspire thousands of inventors, dreamers and engineers.
To this very day, his inventions continue to shape life as we know it.

Tesla invented, predicted or contributed to development of hundreds of technologies that play big parts in our daily lives -- like the remote control, neon and fluorescent lights, wireless transmission, computers, smartphones, laser beams, x-rays, robotics and, of course, alternating current, the basis of our present-day electrical system.

Early Life

Nikola Tesla was born in 1856 in Smiljan, Croatia, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
Tesla was the fourth child in a family of five children. The family comprised of three sisters including Marica, Angelina, Milka and his old brother who was known as Dane.
Dane died when Tesla was still at a tender age. The shock of the loss unsettled the 7-year-old Tesla.

Nikola Tesla is believed to have been born at midnight. During his birth, lightning struck during a summer storm. Born to a Serbian Family in Smiljan, Tesla was nicknamed `Child of the Storm`, by his aunt. That said, his mother replied to that comment by calling him `Child of the light`.

Tesla attended his early education in 1861 in smiljan, where he acquired various skills including arithmetic and he also mastered the German Language. The following year, Nikola Tesla moved to a place known as Gospic. Here, his father played the role of a Parish Pastor.

In 1875, Tesla enrolled to a Polytechnic based on a military scholarship. He was a disciplined student and achieved the highest grades possible while passing various exams.
Tesla studied math and physics at the Technical University of Graz and philosophy at the University of Prague.

Here's a Timeline on the Notable Events of Nikola Tesla's Life:

  • 1859 - On July 10th, Nikola Tesla was born in Smiljan, Croatia.
  • 1870 - Tesla had a near death experience when he contracted Cholera and was hospitalized for close to a year.
  • 1875 - Tesla enrolled to a Polytechnic based on a military scholarship.
  • 1880 - He moves to Prague to study Philosophy.
  • 1881 - Nikola Tesla gets his first electrical job as a Chief Electrical Engineer at the Budapest Telephone Exchange company.
  • 1882 - He found another job in Paris with a company referred to as the Continental Edison Company, a company owned by Thomas Edison.
  • 1884 - Tesla moved to New York in the USA.
  • 1885 - Tesla, with the help of an attorney known as Lemuel Serrel, submits some of his first patents.
  • 1887 - Tesla came up with an induction motor that was operated by Alternating Current
  • 1890 - Tesla begins his work on Wireless Power transmission
  • 1891 - At the age of 35, Tesla became a Naturalized citizen of US. In the same year he also patented his famous Tesla Coil
  • 1943 - In early 1943, at age 86, Tesla died in a Hotel Room in New York.
